Venue

TNSAA 2026 will be held at the RIKEN Center for Computational Science (R-CCS) in Kobe, Japan.

RIKEN Center for Computational Science (R-CCS)#

RIKEN Center for Computational Science (R-CCS) 7-1-26 Minatojima-minamimachi, Chuo-ku, Kobe, Hyogo 650-0047, Japan

R-CCS is one of Japan’s leading research centers for high-performance computing and computational science. It is located on Port Island in Kobe, a major international port city in western Japan.

For official access information, please refer to the RIKEN R-CCS directions page.

RIKEN R-CCS Directions

Access#

Nearest Station#

The nearest station is Keisan Kagaku Center Station on the Port Liner line.

R-CCS is located about a 3-minute walk from Keisan Kagaku Center Station.

Please note that there is no visitor parking at R-CCS. Participants are encouraged to use public transportation.

From Sannomiya Station#

From Sannomiya Station, take the Port Liner bound for Kobe Airport and get off at Keisan Kagaku Center Station.

The ride from Sannomiya to Keisan Kagaku Center Station takes approximately 15 minutes.

Please take the Port Liner bound for Kobe Airport, not the line bound for Kita Futo.

From Shin-Kobe Station#

From Shin-Kobe Station, take the Kobe City Subway to Sannomiya Station. At Sannomiya, transfer to the Port Liner bound for Kobe Airport and get off at Keisan Kagaku Center Station.

From Kobe Airport#

From Kobe Airport, take the Port Liner toward Sannomiya and get off at Keisan Kagaku Center Station.

The ride from Kobe Airport to Keisan Kagaku Center Station takes approximately 5 minutes.

From Kansai International Airport (KIX)#

From Kansai International Airport, participants may take an airport limousine bus or train connection to Sannomiya, and then transfer to the Port Liner bound for Kobe Airport.

Please check the latest schedules and routes before traveling.

Accommodation#

Participants are recommended to stay near Sannomiya or nearby central Kobe areas, as these areas provide convenient access to the Port Liner and to restaurants, shops, and transportation.

Recommended areas include:

  • Sannomiya
  • Motomachi
  • Kobe Harborland
  • Shin-Kobe

Hotel information and recommended options may be announced later.

Visa Information#

Participants who require an invitation letter for visa application purposes should indicate this in the registration form.

The organizing committee will contact participants who request visa support after their registration has been submitted.

Please note that visa support documents can only be issued to registered participants.

For further inquiries, please refer to the Contact page.

About Kobe#

Kobe is an international port city located in western Japan. It is known for its waterfront, modern architecture, international atmosphere, and convenient access from major transportation hubs in the Kansai region.

The city offers a wide range of restaurants, hotels, and cultural attractions, making it a convenient and pleasant location for international workshops.
